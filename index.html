    What is a Data Breach?

    A data breach occurs when sensitive or confidential information is exposed or stolen. This can include your email address, which, if compromised, can lead to further security risks.

    What to Do If Your Email Was Breached

    If the check indicates your email has been involved in a breach, here are crucial steps you should take immediately:

    • Change Your Passwords: Update the password for the breached account and, importantly, for any other accounts where you use the same password. Choose strong, unique passwords for each.
    • Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Add an extra layer of security to your important accounts (like email, banking, social media) by enabling 2FA whenever possible.
    • Be Alert for Phishing: Be extra cautious about any emails or messages you receive, especially those asking for personal information or containing links. Breached emails are often targets for phishing attacks.
    • Review Account Activity: Check your online accounts for any suspicious activity or unauthorized transactions.
    • Consider a Password Manager: A password manager can help you generate and securely store strong, unique passwords for all your accounts.

    How to Protect Yourself Online (Proactive Steps)

    Even if your email hasn't been breached, taking these proactive steps can significantly improve your online security:

    • Use Strong, Unique Passwords: This cannot be stressed enough. Aim for passwords that are at least 12 characters long and include a mix of uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols.
    • Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Make this a standard practice for all accounts that offer it.
    • Be Skeptical of Unsolicited Communications: Don't click on links or open attachments from unknown or suspicious senders.
    • Keep Your Software Updated: Ensure your operating system, browser, and apps are always up to date to patch security vulnerabilities.
    • Use a Reputable Security Software: Install and maintain antivirus and anti-malware software on your devices.
    • Be Mindful of Public Wi-Fi: Avoid accessing sensitive accounts on public Wi-Fi or use a VPN for added security.
